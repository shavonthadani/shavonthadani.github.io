Frequently Asked Questions


Depending on the size, breed, and coat condition of your dog, a grooming session can take anywhere from one to three hours.

The frequency of grooming depends on your dog's breed, hair length, and lifestyle. As a general rule, most dogs should be groomed every four to six weeks, but dogs with longer hair may require more frequent or professional grooming.

It's common for dogs to be anxious about grooming, especially if they're not used to it. We recommend bringing a favorite toy or blanket to comfort your dog. Also, spending some time getting your dog used to being handled and touched can be helpful. This includes touching your dog's paws, which can make nail trimming easier.

Before coming to the grooming session, make sure your dog has had plenty of exercises so they're more relaxed. Also, it's a good idea to brush your dog to remove any mats or tangles.

After grooming, we recommend waiting for a while before you let your dog jump into a pool or play in the mud. Besides, if your dog was trimmed or shaved, limit their exposure to extreme sun to prevent sunburn. Remember that the best source of information about your pet's grooming needs is your groomer - don't hesitate to reach out with any specific concerns or questions about your pet's care!
